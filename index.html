The most important roots

Jerusalem is the third holiest place for Muslims after Mecca and Medina, and it represented the qibla of Islamic prayer for nearly a year, before the qibla changed to the Kaaba in Mecca. Jerusalem became a city of religious importance to Muslims after the angel Gabriel took the Prophet Muhammad captive there, according to Islamic belief, around the year 620, where he ascended from the holy rock to the highest heavens, where he met all the prophets and messengers who preceded him and received from God the teachings of prayer and how to perform it. Surah Al-Isra states that Muhammad was taken on captivity from the Sacred Mosque to Al-Aqsa Mosque.

Bethlehem has great importance to Christians as it is the birthplace of Jesus (Isa). There are many churches in Bethlehem, perhaps the most important of which is the Church of the Nativity, which was built by Constantine the Great (330 AD). This is above a cave or grotto, which is believed to be the stable in which Jesus was born. It is believed that this church is the oldest existing church in the world. There is also another crypt nearby where Jerome is believed to have spent thirty years of his life translating the Bible.

Jericho is an ancient historical Palestinian city that dates back to 10,000 BC. It is the capital of Jericho Governorate and it is the oldest city in history, dating back to the Stone Age, 10-11 thousand years ago, that is, about before the eighth millennium BC. In the Middle Bronze Age, specifically in the first half of the second millennium BC, Jericho grew as a major urban center with its palaces, fortification system, and cemeteries.

Roots In Palestine

Palestine stands at the forefront of countries rich in historical, archaeological and religious sites, which constitute an important part of the Palestinian cultural identity and an integral part of the human cultural heritage. In the Palestinian territories in the West Bank and Gaza Strip, there are more than (2000) archaeological sites.

Historical, Archaeological And Religious Monuments

Hisham's Palace - Ancient Jericho: also known as Khirbat al-Mafjar, is an important early Islamic archaeological site in the Palestinian city of Jericho, in the West Bank. Built by the Umayyad dynasty in the first half of the 8th century, it is one of the so-called Umayyad desert castles.

The Dead Sea - Jericho: It is a salt lake bordered by Jordan to the east and Palestine's West Bank and Israel to the west. It lies in the Jordan Rift Valley, and its main tributary is the Jordan River

Wadi Qelt - Ancient Jericho: in Hebrew Nahal Prat, formerly Naḥal Faran, is a valley, riverine gulch or stream in the West Bank, originating near Jerusalem and running into the Jordan River near Jericho, shortly before it flows into the Dead Sea.

9 mins

Sebastia - Nablus: An important archaeological site with many archaeological sites still standing, including: the Western Gate, the Street of Columns, the Central Square, the Roman Amphitheater, the Greek Tower, the Temple of Augustus, and the Palace of King Omri.

9 mins

Roman Amphitheater - Nablus: Its construction dates back to the third century AD, and it was discovered in 1980 AD. It was a place for wrestling for life between the combatants and between them and predatory animals.

9 mins

Tell Sultan - Ancient Jericho: also known as Tel Jericho or Ancient Jericho, is an archaeological site and a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the State of Palestine, in the city of Jericho, consisting of the remains of the oldest fortified city in the world.

9 mins

Alqiama charch - Jerusalem: The Church of the Resurrection is considered the oldest, most important and most sacred in the Christian world. It was built over the tomb of Jesus Christ. The construction of the church dates back to the fourth century AD and it took eleven years to build.

9 mins

Damascus Gate - Jerusalem: It is located in the middle of the northern wall of the Jerusalem Wall and dates back to the era of the Ottoman Sultan Suleiman the Magnificent. Above this door is a round arch standing between two towers.

9 mins

Dome of Spirits - Jerusalem: It is located to the north of the Dome of the Rock in the Holy Mosque. It dates back to the tenth century AH, and it may have been named that because of its proximity to the cave known as the Cave of Souls. The dome consists of a structure made of eight marble columns supported by eight pointed arches.

Most prominent religious monuments.

Jerusalem

Dome of the Rock

Mon, May 25th 2023

The Dome of the Rock was built in the heart of the Holy Mosque, on the northern side, opposite the blessed Al-Aqsa Mosque, over the rock from which the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) ascended to the highest heavens.

Bethlehem

Church of the Nativity

Mon, May 25th 2023

The Church of the Nativity, or Basilica of the Nativity, is a basilica located in Bethlehem in the State of Palestine, in the West Bank. The grotto holds a prominent religious significance to Christians of various denominations as the birthplace of Jesus.

Jerusalem

Al-Aqsa Mosque

Mon, May 25th 2023

The Aqsa Mosque, also known as the Qibli Mosque or Qibli Chapel, is the main congregational mosque or prayer hall in the Al-Aqsa mosque compound in the Old City of Jerusalem.

Hebron

Ibrahimi Mosque

Mon, May 25th 2023

The Cave of the Patriarchs or Tomb of the Patriarchs, known to Jews by its Biblical name Cave of Machpelah and to Muslims as the Sanctuary of Abraham, is a series of caves situated 30 kilometres south of Jerusalem in the heart of the Old City of Hebron in the West Bank
